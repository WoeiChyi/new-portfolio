Work Zone Digital Twin for Detecting Worker Violating Geofence

Digital Twin Wearable Sensing Safety Unity C#
Problem
  • Work zone intrusions have been a safety-critical issue in transportation and construction.
  • Besides vehicle-related factors, worker violating geofence must be considered a contributing factor.
Method
  • Developed a work zone digital twin aggregating data from channelizing devices, beacon, and GPS receivers.
  • Conducted real-time detection of workers violating geofence area.
  • Validated through a pilot test in a simulated road work zone.
Result
  • Detection accuracy of 50 ± 11 cm.
  • Detection latency of 0.15 ± 0.01 s.
Impact
  • Enables identification of workers' risk-taking behaviors on work zones.
  • Builds a foundation to combine vehicle-related monitoring and proactive alerts to improve safety.

Digital Twin Wearable Sensing Human-Centered ML/DL Unity C#

Work Zone Intrusion Risk Assessment through LLMs and Deep Learning

Risk Assessment LLMs Safety TensorFlow Python
Problem
  • Work zone intrusions are a multi-dimensional, complex issue.
  • Lack of risk assessment due to limited available databases.
Method
  • Retrieved crash reports from ARIES database including tabular, resultant, and narrative data.
  • Likelihood assessment using GPT-4o to identify features aligned with drivers' self-reported causes.
  • Severity assessment using a deep learning model classifying incidents as high or low severity.
Result
  • Top likelihood factors: aggressive driving, lane changes, slippery surfaces.
  • Top severity factors: vehicles being towed, hit-and-run, one-way-one-lane roads.
Impact
  • Helps stakeholders better assess risk based on historical reports.
  • Supports proactive implementation of preventive strategies to mitigate intrusion risk.

Physiology-Based Modeling and Prediction of Human-Autonomy Trust

Human Factors Physiology Sensing Deep Learning VR/AR/XR Unity C# Python TensorFlow
Problem
  • Industries are changing via tech integration, raising concern about workforce readiness for interacting with technologies.
  • Lack of knowledge on how workers build trust in autonomous technologies within construction.
Method
  • Designed a VR-based human-autonomy bricklaying experiment in Unity with 105 participants.
  • Deployed physiology sensing (brain activation, eye-tracking, EDA, heart rate).
  • Conducted statistical analyses (t-test, ANOVA, correlation) to understand trust drivers.
  • Developed a CNN-LSTM hybrid model to predict human trust dynamics.
Result
  • Trust dynamically fluctuated with failures identified and risk perception, reflected by prefrontal cortex activation.
  • Model achieved 71.9% accuracy, 74.4% precision, 75.8% recall, 75.1% F1-score.
Impact
  • Concludes that failure to identify failures leads to overtrust and misuse of autonomous systems.
  • Demonstrates prefrontal cortex activation as an objective measure of trust.
  • Enables trust-aware autonomous systems that adapt to workers' cognitive states in real time.

Intrusion Prevention Technologies Usability Analysis through Driving Experiments

User Experience Experimentation Physiological Sensing Excel VBA
Problem
  • Lack of empirical evaluation of commercially available intrusion prevention technologies from a driver-centered perspective.
Method
  • Conducted driving experiments to assess 12 drivers' responses to SonoBlaster, WAS, AWARE, and Intellicone.
  • Evaluated using attention (eye-tracking), brain activation (fNIRS), stress (Empatica), reaction time, and stoppage distance.
  • Developed an interactive dashboard integrating quantitative and qualitative driver feedback.
Result
  • WAS and Intellicone demonstrated stronger driver responsiveness and shorter reaction times.
  • Intellicone rated most usable and least stressful; WAS showed higher situational awareness via fNIRS.
Impact
  • Provides DOTs with driver-centered insights to guide technology selection.
  • Delivers a decision-support tool comparing technologies on cost, effectiveness, and human factors.
